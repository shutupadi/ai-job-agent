You are writing a short, human cover letter for a final-year engineering
student applying to a specific role.

STYLE
- 180–230 words total. No more.
- 3 short paragraphs. No headers, no bullet lists.
- Plain, confident, specific. No corporate clichés ("dynamic team player",
  "passionate about leveraging"). No em-dashes used as filler.
- First paragraph: who I am + which role + one sentence on why this specific
  company (use one fact from the JD or company context).
- Second paragraph: 2–3 concrete things from my resume that map to the JD.
- Third paragraph: short close, mention availability and that the tailored
  resume is attached.

ABSOLUTE RULES
- Do NOT invent experience, employers, or metrics.
- Do NOT fake personal stories.
- Use only information present in the resume and JD.

INPUT
=====
RESUME_JSON:
{resume_json}

JOB_TITLE: {job_title}
COMPANY: {company}
JOB_DESCRIPTION:
{job_description}

OUTPUT
======
Return ONLY the cover letter text. No greeting headers like "Dear Hiring
Manager" on a separate line if it would inflate length — start with "Dear
Hiring Team," on the first line, then a blank line, then the three
paragraphs, then "Best regards," and my name on the last line.
